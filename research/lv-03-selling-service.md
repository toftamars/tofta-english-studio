# Louis Vuitton — Selling Ceremony, Client Experience & After-Sales

## Part 1 — Research Findings (Cross-Checked)

### 1.1 What is the "Selling Ceremony"?
Louis Vuitton and other luxury houses use the term **"selling ceremony"** to describe a standardized, step-by-step client experience. It is *intentional*: every word, gesture, and product presentation has a purpose — to make the client feel **valued, understood, and delighted**, not just "served." LV trains this through daily briefings, 1:1 coaching, and role-play, and Expert Client Advisors **observe the team during the selling ceremony** to give feedback. ([Retail for the People][1]; [LinkedIn — 12 Steps][3]; [LVMH — Expert Client Advisor][6])

### 1.2 The Client Journey / Selling Ceremony Steps
Cross-checking several sources, the journey follows this sequence:
**Welcome → Discovery (needs) → Product presentation & storytelling → Handling objections → Add-on / cross-sell → Closing → Payment → Packaging → Farewell → Follow-up.**
- **Welcome:** warm, genuine, personalized; greet by name if known, offer a beverage. ([Retail for the People][1]; [LinkedIn][3])
- **Discovery:** "lose the script," ask **open-ended questions**, listen ~80% of the time, find the *occasion and emotion* behind the purchase. ([Sonali Gogia][5]; [Pulse Sales Trainings][4])
- **Presentation:** **storytelling** about craftsmanship and heritage; **demonstrate value, not price**; let the client touch and try. ([Retail for the People][1]; [LinkedIn][3])
- **Objections:** deal with them positively, never pushy; respect the client's intelligence. ([Selling Luxury — Wiley][2])
- **Closing & aftercare:** affirm the client's decision; the relationship continues after the sale — *"The sale ends when the client leaves. The relationship begins when you call."* ([Pulse][4]; [Retail Insider][7])

### 1.3 Clienteling & CRM
Clienteling is *"the art of knowing your client and showing them you care."* It means **capturing client data** (name, contact, preferences, milestones), **personalized non-automated follow-up**, logging special requests, and cultivating **VIP / VIC clients** through appointments and exclusive experiences. LV uses internal tools (e.g., **ICON, MyCC, DREAM**) so advisors keep consistent records and follow up. ([Retail Insider][7]; [LVMH — Client Development Manager][8]; [Pulse][4])

### 1.4 Service Standards & "The LV Experience"
- **Body language:** open, relaxed posture; appropriate eye contact; nodding; never crossed arms. ([Giva][9])
- **Attentiveness & discretion:** read what the client says *and does not say*; give space when needed. ([Luxury Learnings][10]; [Luxury Daily][11])
- **Authenticity over script:** clients detect "performed" attention; genuine presence and real listening win. ([Luxury Daily][11]; [Support Services Group][12])

### 1.5 After-Sales
Repairs, exchanges, and complaints are **relationship opportunities**. Stay calm, listen actively, use empathy, resolve swiftly, and turn a complaint into deeper trust. ([Luxury Learnings][10]; [Luxury Learnings Dubai][13])

## Part 2 — ⭐ Ready-to-Use English Phrases by Step (with Türkçe)

> Tip for Hülya: Speak slowly and smile. Short sentences are perfect at A2.

### Step 1 — Welcome / Greeting (Karşılama)
| English | Türkçe |
|---|---|
| Hello, welcome to Louis Vuitton! | Merhaba, Louis Vuitton'a hoş geldiniz! |
| Good afternoon. How are you today? | İyi günler. Bugün nasılsınız? |
| Please, come in and look around. | Lütfen, içeri buyurun ve etrafa bakının. |
| My name is Hülya. I am here to help you. | Benim adım Hülya. Size yardımcı olmak için buradayım. |
| Can I offer you some water, coffee, or tea? | Size su, kahve ya da çay ikram edebilir miyim? |
| Take your time, please. | Lütfen, acele etmeyin. |

### Step 2 — Discovery / Needs (Keşif — açık uçlu sorular)
| English | Türkçe |
|---|---|
| What are you looking for today? | Bugün ne arıyorsunuz? |
| Is it for you, or is it a gift? | Kendiniz için mi, yoksa hediye mi? |
| What do you like — a bag, a wallet, or shoes? | Ne seversiniz — çanta, cüzdan ya da ayakkabı? |
| What colors do you usually wear? | Genellikle hangi renkleri giyersiniz? |
| Do you prefer something classic or modern? | Klasik bir şey mi yoksa modern bir şey mi tercih edersiniz? |
| For what occasion will you use it? | Bunu hangi durum için kullanacaksınız? |

### Step 3 — Product Presentation & Storytelling (Ürün sunumu ve hikâye)
| English | Türkçe |
|---|---|
| Let me show you this beautiful bag. | Size bu güzel çantayı göstereyim. |
| This is one of our most famous designs. | Bu, en ünlü tasarımlarımızdan biri. |

| This monogram is a symbol of the Maison's history. | Bu monogram, Maison'un tarihinin bir simgesidir. |
| Please, feel free to touch it and try it. | Lütfen, dokunabilir ve deneyebilirsiniz. |

### Step 4 — Handling Objections (İtirazları karşılama)
| English | Türkçe |
|---|---|
| I understand. Let me explain a little more. | Anlıyorum. Biraz daha açıklayayım. |

| Of course, take your time to decide. | Tabii ki, karar vermek için acele etmeyin. |
| Would you like to compare it with another model? | Başka bir modelle karşılaştırmak ister misiniz? |
| No problem. Let me show you another option. | Sorun değil. Size başka bir seçenek göstereyim. |
| That is a very good question. | Bu çok güzel bir soru. |

## Part 3 — Difficult Situations & Complaints (Zor durumlar ve şikâyetler)

> Stay calm, listen, and never argue. Show empathy first, then solve the problem.

| English | Türkçe |
|---|---|
| I am very sorry to hear that. | Bunu duyduğuma çok üzüldüm. |
| I understand how you feel. | Nasıl hissettiğinizi anlıyorum. |
| Thank you for telling me. Let me help you. | Söylediğiniz için teşekkürler. Size yardımcı olayım. |
| Let me check this for you right now. | Hemen sizin için kontrol edeyim. |
| I am sorry for the inconvenience. | Verdiğimiz rahatsızlık için özür dilerim. |
| We can repair it for you. | Onu sizin için tamir edebiliriz. |
| Would you like an exchange or a repair? | Değişim mi yoksa tamir mi istersiniz? |
| Let me ask my manager to help you. | Size yardımcı olması için yöneticimden rica edeyim. |
| Please give me one moment to fix this. | Bunu düzeltmem için lütfen bir dakika izin verin. |
| Thank you for your patience. | Sabrınız için teşekkür ederim. |

## Part 4 — Glossary (Sözlük · 18 terms)

| Term | Simple definition | Türkçe |
|---|---|---|
| Client advisor | A salesperson in a luxury store who helps and advises clients. | Müşteri danışmanı |
| Selling ceremony | The step-by-step way of serving a client, from welcome to farewell. | Satış seremonisi |
| Greeting | The first words you say to welcome a client. | Karşılama / selamlama |
| Discovery | Finding out what the client wants by asking questions. | Keşif (ihtiyaç belirleme) |
| Open question | A question with a long answer, not just "yes/no." | Açık uçlu soru |
| Needs | The things the client wants or is looking for. | İhtiyaçlar |
| Storytelling | Telling the story and history of a product. | Hikâye anlatımı |
| Craftsmanship | High-quality work made carefully by hand. | El işçiliği / ustalık |
| Heritage | The long history and tradition of the brand. | Miras / köklü tarih |
| Objection | A reason the client gives for not buying. | İtiraz |
| Cross-sell / add-on | Offering an extra item that goes with the main one. | Çapraz satış / tamamlayıcı ürün |
| Closing | The moment the client decides to buy. | Satışı kapatma |
| Receipt | The paper that shows the client paid. | Fiş / makbuz |
| Packaging | Putting the product in a box and bag. | Paketleme |
| Farewell | Saying goodbye politely. | Uğurlama / veda |
| Follow-up | Contacting the client again after the visit. | Takip / sonradan iletişim |
| Clienteling | Building a long relationship and remembering each client. | Müşteri ilişkisi yönetimi |
| VIP / VIC | A very important client who gets special service. | Çok önemli müşteri |
