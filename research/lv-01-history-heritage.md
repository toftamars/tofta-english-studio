## 1. Founding & The Founder (Louis Vuitton, 1821–1892)

- **Louis Vuitton** was born on **4 August 1821** in the village of **Anchay**, in the **Jura** region of eastern France, near the Swiss border. He came from a modest, working-class family of millers and carpenters. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; Louis Vuitton "The Essentials")*
- His mother died when he was young. At about **age 13**, he left home for Paris — a journey of roughly **470 km that he largely made on foot**, taking about two years and arriving around **1837**. *(Cross-checked: la-malle-en-coin; biography.com)*
- In Paris he became an apprentice (a *layetier-emballeur*, i.e. a box-maker and packer) under the master craftsman **Monsieur Maréchal**, learning to build custom boxes and to pack delicate, valuable goods. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; biography.com; LV Essentials)*
- He gained prestige as the **personal trunk-maker and packer for Empress Eugénie de Montijo**, wife of **Napoleon III**. This royal connection opened the door to other elite and aristocratic clients. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; la-malle-en-coin; biography.com)*
- In **1854**, Louis married **Clémence-Émilie Parriaux** (on 22 April 1854) and **founded his own Maison the same year**, opening a workshop in Paris at **4 Rue Neuve-des-Capucines**, near the Place Vendôme. His shop sign read: *"Securely packs the most fragile objects. Specializing in packing fashions."* *(Cross-checked: LV Essentials; Christie's; biography.com)*
- Louis Vuitton **died on 27 February 1892**, aged 70. The company passed to his son **Georges Vuitton**. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; biography.com)*

### Trunk-making origins & key innovations

- **The flat-top trunk (1858):** Vuitton introduced a **rectangular, flat-topped trunk** covered in **Trianon gray canvas** — lightweight, airtight, water-resistant, and **stackable**. Until then, trunks had **rounded (dome-shaped) tops** so water would run off; Vuitton's flat design was revolutionary because it could be stacked for the new age of **railroads and steamships**. Many historians call this **the birth of modern luggage**. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; Christie's; biography.com)*
- **Asnières-sur-Seine workshop (1859):** As demand grew, the atelier moved to **Asnières**, just outside Paris, where it **still operates today** as the historic heart of the Maison's *savoir-faire*. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; LV Careers)*
- **Anti-counterfeiting through canvas designs.** Because his trunks were copied, the Maison kept evolving its signature canvas:
  - **Striped (rayé) canvas — 1872** (red/white, later beige/brown).
  - **Damier (checkerboard) canvas — 1888**, printed with *"marque L. Vuitton déposée"* ("L. Vuitton registered trademark").
  - **Monogram canvas — 1896.**
  *(Cross-checked: Christie's; Wikipedia; LV Essentials; Brandvelle)*
- **The Monogram canvas (1896):** Created by **Georges Vuitton** as a **tribute to his late father**. It combines the interlaced **"LV" initials** with a **four-petal flower, a four-pointed star, and a quatrefoil**, motifs inspired by the late-Victorian fashion for **Japanese family crests (*mon*)**. It is now one of the most recognized — and most copied — patterns in fashion. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; Christie's; Brandvelle; LVMH)*
- **The tumbler (picking-proof) lock:** Georges Vuitton developed a **single, secure lock system** in the 1880s, perfected and registered around **1888–1890**, with a **5-tumbler** mechanism that let a client open **all of their luggage with one personal numbered key**. It was considered "unpickable." Famously, Georges **challenged escape artist Harry Houdini** to break out of a locked Vuitton trunk — Houdini never took up the challenge. The lock remains a hallmark of LV trunks today. *(Cross-checked: Christie's; LV Careers; Baggage Collection)*

> Note on dates (paranoid check): sources differ slightly on the lock — Christie's cites a single-lock system "1886," LV's own pages cite the multiple tumbler lock "1887, perfected and registered 1889," and the Baggage Collection cites the "V" tumbler lock "1890, registered around 1905." The reliable, agreed core fact is **late 1880s, 5-tumbler, one-key system, registered ~1888–1890**.

## 2. Major Milestones (19th–21st Century)

- **1867:** Louis Vuitton exhibits at the **Universal Exhibition (Exposition Universelle)** in Paris. *(Wikipedia)*
- **1885:** Opens its **first store outside France**, on **Oxford Street, London**. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; Christie's)*
- **1892:** Louis Vuitton dies; **Georges Vuitton** takes over and builds the brand worldwide. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; biography.com)*
- **1896:** Launch of the **Monogram canvas**, later patented internationally. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; Christie's; Brandvelle)*
- **1901:** Introduces the **Steamer Bag**, a soft bag kept inside the trunk — an early ancestor of the modern handbag. *(Wikipedia)*
- **1914:** Opens the **Louis Vuitton Building** on the **Champs-Élysées** in Paris, then the largest travel-goods store in the world. *(Wikipedia)*
- **Mid–20th century:** The Maison expands beyond trunks into **handbags, small leather goods, and accessories**. *(Wikipedia)*
- **1987:** **Creation of LVMH** (see Section 3).
- **1997:** **Marc Jacobs** appointed; the brand introduces its **first ready-to-wear** clothing line (see Section 4).
- **1998–2000s:** Global expansion into fashion, watches, jewelry, and fragrance; high-profile **artist collaborations** begin.
- **2023:** LVMH becomes the **first European company to surpass a $500 billion valuation**, underlining Louis Vuitton's role as its flagship Maison. *(Wikipedia / LVMH)*

## 3. Joining LVMH (1987 Merger)

- **LVMH (Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton)** was created on **3 June 1987**, through the **merger of Louis Vuitton with Moët Hennessy**. *(Cross-checked: LVMH official; UPI 1987 archive; Wikipedia)*
- **Moët Hennessy** itself was formed in **1971** by merging the champagne house **Moët & Chandon** (founded 1743) and the cognac house **Hennessy** (founded 1765). *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; LVMH)*
- At creation, the Group had about **10 Maisons, 12,000 employees, and €3 billion in sales**. *(LVMH official)*
- After the merger, the leaders of the two original firms — **Alain Chevalier** (Moët Hennessy) and **Henry Racamier** (Louis Vuitton) — clashed over control. Racamier invited investor **Bernard Arnault** to take a stake. *(Cross-checked: Wikipedia; FundingUniverse)*
- **Bernard Arnault** then acquired a controlling interest and, by **January 1989**, became **Chairman and CEO** — a role he still holds. He built LVMH into the world's leading luxury group. *(Cross-checked: LVMH official; Wikipedia; FundingUniverse)*

## 5. Brand Values, Maison Philosophy & "Savoir-Faire"

- **The Maison.** Louis Vuitton calls itself a **"Maison"** (French for "House") — not just a company, but a family-rooted house of craft with a continuous identity since 1854. *(LVMH; LV Careers)*
- **Savoir-faire.** This French phrase means **expert craftsmanship and know-how passed down through generations**, from master artisan to apprentice. The historic **Asnières atelier** is described as the **"cradle of Louis Vuitton's savoir-faire."** *(Cross-checked: LV Careers; Harper's Bazaar; LV YouTube)*
- **The Art of Travel.** The founding idea: travel is not just a destination but **a philosophy and a way of life**. From trunks built for sea voyages to today's bags and accessories, LV aims to **accompany the client on every journey** — physical and creative. *(Cross-checked: LVMH; LV YouTube; LV Careers)*
- **Innovation + tradition.** The brand's motto in spirit: an **"uncompromising demand for excellence,"** combining **traditional craft with a relentless spirit of innovation** (from the unpickable lock to connected watches). *(Cross-checked: LVMH; LV Careers)*
- **Exclusivity & rarity.** The Monogram became "a sign of distinction and rarity"; products are positioned as **treasures of savoir-faire**, made to last and to be passed on. *(Cross-checked: LVMH; Harper's Bazaar)*
- **Heritage that is still alive.** The House has **never stopped making travel trunks since 1854**; each trunk is still largely handmade and can take **dozens of hours** to produce, with a unique serial number. *(Cross-checked: Harper's Bazaar; Wikipedia)*

## 6. Iconic Brand-Story Talking Points (for Client Advisors)

Short, true, memorable lines an advisor can share with a customer:
1. "Our Maison was founded in **Paris in 1854** by Louis Vuitton, a master trunk-maker."
2. "Louis Vuitton was the **personal packer for the Empress of France** — luxury and trust from the very beginning."
3. "In **1858** he invented the **flat-top trunk** — it was the start of **modern luggage**."
4. "The famous **Monogram canvas** was created in **1896** by his son Georges, as a **tribute to his father**."
5. "Our trunk **lock cannot be picked** — even **Houdini** never tried to escape from it."
6. "Every trunk is still made by hand at our historic **Asnières** atelier, just like in the 19th century."
7. "We don't just sell bags — we offer the **Art of Travel**, a piece that travels with you for life."
8. "This is not only fashion; it is **savoir-faire** — craft passed down through generations."
